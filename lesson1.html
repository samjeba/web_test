About Moon

The Moon (Latin: Luna) is Earth's only natural satellite. Although not the largest natural satellite in the Solar System, it is, among the satellites of major planets, the largest relative to the size of the object it orbits (its primary).

Facts on Moon

  • Distance to Earth: 384,400 km
  • Gravity: 1.622 m/s²
  • Orbital period: 27 days
  • Age: 4.527 billion years
  • Declination: 28° 0.000'
